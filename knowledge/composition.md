# Composition

What to generate into the new space. This guides the extension prompt. Rewrite it
and the output changes — this is the knowledge-as-extension-surface bet. (A
photographer's version and an architect's version differ mostly in this file.)

## The one rule

**The extension is background, not new content.** You are enlarging the world
around the subject, not adding to the story. Sky, ground, wall, foliage, bokeh,
atmosphere — continuation, not invention.

## Continue, don't invent

- **Carry lines through.** A horizon, a road, a railing, a shoreline, a table
  edge — continue it at the _same angle and height_ across the new region. A
  horizon that steps or tilts at the seam is the most common tell.
- **Continue perspective.** Vanishing points and convergence established in the
  original must keep converging into the extension. Don't flatten or re-angle.
- **Extend texture, vary it.** Grass, gravel, water, foliage should continue as
  the _same material_ — but avoid a tiled, repeating stamp. Natural variation,
  same substance.

## Keep the subject anchored

- **No new focal subject.** Do not add a person, animal, building, or object
  that competes for attention. If the original is a portrait against sky, the
  extension is _more sky_, not a second figure.
- **Respect the frame's intent.** If the subject sits on a third, extending the
  empty side should preserve that breathing room, not center the subject.
- **Honor negative space.** Empty, quiet areas are often the point of an
  extension (room for a headline at 21:9). Keep them calm.

## Scale and depth

- Objects added near the original's foreground read large; toward the horizon,
  small. Keep the size gradient consistent with the original's depth cues.
- Match depth-of-field: if the background is soft, the extended background stays
  soft. Don't render a crisp far field behind a shallow-focus subject.
